From this data exploration and analysis, it appears that there is no significant relationship between university ranks and acceptance rates and seems to vary by region. This could also vary with the criteria used to base the ranking on. For example, if the critieria were based on the number of output of something (for example incorporating the quanitiy of published papers), a school with a higher acceptance rate and larger student body may thrive. However, if we were basing the rank on some indicator of average performance of students after university, universities with a lower acceptance rate and more selective admissions process may thrive. Due to the subjectivity of the success of a university and the lack of significant correlation seen from our data exploration, it seems that using acceptance rates as an indicator of the success and quality of university is a flawed mentality that many hold.
